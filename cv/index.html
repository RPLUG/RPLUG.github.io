Professional Summary

Ph.D. student in Chemical Engineering at the Institute of Process Engineering, Chinese Academy of Sciences, working on multi-GPU parallel computing, discrete element modelling, multiphysics coupling, and AI-assisted experiment-simulation integration. Developer of CUDA/MPI scientific software for large-scale complex-particle systems, with research spanning fundamental algorithms and industrial particulate processes. Participated in industrial research projects with China Coal, Baosteel, and Unilever.

Experience

  • 2022 - present

    Beijing, China

    Large-Scale DEM and GPU-Accelerated Computing
    Institute of Process Engineering, Chinese Academy of Sciences
    • Developed a CUDA/MPI-based multi-GPU DEM framework for concave and convex polyhedral particles
    • Enabled simulations of 20 million complex particles represented by approximately 12 billion triangular facets
    • Achieved near-linear weak scaling and over 80 percent strong-scaling efficiency on 16 NVIDIA RTX 4090 GPUs
    • Applied the framework to rotating drums, silo discharge, fixed beds, and particle packing
  • 2025 - present

    Beijing, China

    Polyhedral-Particle CFD-DEM and Multiscale Gas-Solid Modelling
    Institute of Process Engineering, Chinese Academy of Sciences
    • Developed a CPU-GPU heterogeneous coupling framework integrating OpenFOAM with a polyhedral-particle DEM solver
    • Investigated multiscale gas-solid methods including CG-DPM and MP-PIC
    • Targeted fluidized beds, fixed beds, and industrial particle-conveying systems
  • 2024 - 2026

    Beijing, China

    AI-Assisted Particle Measurement and DEM Parameter Identification
    Institute of Process Engineering, Chinese Academy of Sciences
    • Generated synthetic DEM datasets and trained Mask R-CNN models for irregular-particle image segmentation
