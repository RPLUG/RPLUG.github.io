I am a Ph.D. student in Chemical Engineering advised by Prof. Ji Xu and Prof. Wei Ge. My research lies at the intersection of particulate-flow modelling, high-performance computing, and AI-assisted experiment-simulation integration. I develop numerical methods and scientific software for understanding complex particle and particle-fluid systems.

My recent work includes CUDA/MPI-based multi-GPU discrete element method (DEM) frameworks for concave and convex polyhedral particles, CPU-GPU heterogeneous CFD-DEM solvers, multiscale gas-solid modelling, and deep-learning methods for particle tracking and DEM parameter identification. The multi-GPU DEM framework has enabled simulations of up to 20 million complex particles represented by approximately 12 billion triangular facets.

Research focus

  • Large-scale particle simulation: multi-GPU DEM, domain decomposition, and scalable scientific computing.
  • Complex particle and multiphase systems: non-spherical particles, CFD-DEM, CG-DPM, MP-PIC, and MPS.
  • AI for physical modelling: particle segmentation and tracking, velocity-field reconstruction, and contact-parameter inference.
  • Industrial particulate processes: fluidized beds, rotary devices, hoppers, extrusion, and solids handling.

I have worked on academic and industrial research with collaborators from the Chinese Academy of Sciences and industrial partners including Baosteel and Unilever. See the research overview and publication list for details.
